### Long Caption

Hot take: the problem with your AI coding workflow isn't the model. It's the context.

Every LLM operates in two zones: a smart zone at the start of a fresh session, and a dumb zone as the context fills up. By the time you hit roughly 100K tokens, quality drops fast. And it doesn't matter whether you're using a 200K or a 1M context window. You've just been shipped more dumb zone, not more intelligence.

The fix isn't to prompt louder. It's to architect your sessions the way a good engineer architects a system: small, deliberate, and observable.

Here's how the best AI-assisted devs do it:

They start with a Grill Me session, not a plan. The AI interviews them relentlessly until both sides share the same design concept.

They write a PRD after alignment, not instead of it.

They delegate expensive exploration tasks to sub-agents, keeping the main context clean.

They know which tasks need a human in the loop (alignment) and which ones can run AFK overnight (implementation).

The result? Faster shipping, less hallucination, and sessions that don't derail halfway through a feature.
