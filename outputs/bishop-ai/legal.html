Standard documents defining our data handling protocols, value-based project engagement parameters, and compliance guidelines.

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 22, 2026

1. Information We Collect

We collect information you submit directly to us via our contact and download forms. This includes your name, business email address, company name, revenue brackets, and details regarding GTM bottlenecks. We also log analytics data (e.g. browser type, visiting pages, session length) to measure site performance.

2. How We Use Your Data

We process your data exclusively to:

  • Respond to operational inquiries and process scheduling calendar requests.
  • Sync lead records with our Salesforce CRM backend system.
  • Distribute the gated State of AI 2025 report and related newsletters.

3. Data Synchronization & Storage

Our forms link directly with Salesforce databases and middleware systems (n8n). We implement SSL encryption across all database pipelines. We never rent, trade, or sell lead lists to external third parties. Any tracking scripts (Google Analytics/Drift) process information anonymously.

4. Your Rights

You can request to view, edit, or delete your contact records from our databases at any time. Simply email your request to richmond@bishopai.io.

Terms of Service

Effective Date: June 22, 2026

1. Value-Based Engagements

All GTM builds and consulting contracts are executed under fixed, value-based project agreements. We do not maintain standard hourly rate cards. The scope of work, technical requirements, middleware nodes, and delivery stages will be outlined in a separate Master Services Agreement (MSA) signed prior to project kickoff.

2. GTM Staging & Testing

Bishop AI builds and tests all integrations and workflows in sandbox staging environments before migrating code to your live Salesforce/HubSpot database CRM. The client is responsible for providing staging access. Bishop AI is not liable for pipeline disruptions occurring due to API changes initiated by third-party systems without notice.

3. Intellectual Property & Code Ownership

Upon final payment of build contracts, client receives complete ownership of the n8n workflows, database architectures, documentation, and prompt templates designed during the engagement. Bishop AI retains the right to use anonymous design patterns and general middleware templates across future projects.

4. Governing Law

These terms and all MSA agreements are governed by the laws of the state of Delaware without regard to conflict of law principles. Any legal actions must be filed within Delaware courts.
