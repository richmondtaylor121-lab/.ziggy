01 — Logo System

The Mark

The Coca-Cola wordmark is a Spencerian script — one of the world's most recognised pieces of lettering. Handle it with care.

Coca-Cola

Primary — White on Red

The canonical lockup. Use in all primary brand contexts.

Coca-Cola

Reverse — Red on White

For light backgrounds and print applications.

Coca-Cola

Dark — White on Midnight

For dark-themed digital environments only.

Logo Usage Rules

✓

Maintain clear space equal to the cap-height of the "C" on all four sides.

✓

Use only on approved backgrounds: Coca-Cola Red, Pure White, or Midnight.

✕

Never stretch, compress, rotate, or distort the wordmark in any direction.

✕

Never recolour the wordmark, add drop shadows, outlines, or effects.

✕

Never place the logo on busy photography without a colour field backing.

✓

Scale proportionally. Never reproduce below 80px wide in digital contexts.

04 — Brand Voice & Vibe

Feel & Tone

How Coca-Cola feels and how it speaks are inseparable. Both must carry the same warmth.

Brand Vibe

Classic Joyful Timeless Refreshing Universal

"Coca-Cola is the universal language of happiness — a single sip that collapses generations, borders, and moments into one shared feeling."

Tone of Voice

Warm Inclusive Celebratory Optimistic Human

"Whatever the moment — big, small, loud, or quiet — there's always a reason to open happiness."

Example: "No event required. Just a cold Coke and someone worth sharing it with."

Voice Characteristics

Sentence Length

Short and punchy for impact headlines. Warm, flowing sentences for storytelling. Never dense or technical.

Formality

Casual-friendly. Conversational, never corporate. Reads like a message from a trusted friend, not a press release.

Humour

Light, warm, universally inclusive. Never edgy, sarcastic, or at anyone's expense. Wit is welcome; cynicism is not.

Language

Plain, universal words that anyone anywhere can understand. No jargon. No industry speak. The simpler, the better.

05 — Brand Guidelines

The Rules

Consistency builds trust. These rules ensure every brand touchpoint speaks with one voice.

🔴

Logo Usage

Always maintain a clear zone equal to the cap-height of the "C" on all sides. Apply the wordmark only on Coca-Cola Red, Pure White, or Midnight backgrounds. Never alter scale asymmetrically, add visual effects, or reproduce below 80px digital / 15mm print.

🔤

Typography Hierarchy

Playfair Display Bold or Black for hero headlines. Playfair Italic for featured pull quotes. Inter SemiBold for subheadings and UI labels. Inter Regular (16px min) for all body copy. Never mix more than two typefaces on a single page.

🎨

Colour Application

Coca-Cola Red drives primary CTAs, key headlines, and identity moments. Pure White is the primary reverse and breathing surface. Warm Cream for page backgrounds and cards. Stone Grey for metadata. Deep Crimson for hover and active states only.

📷

Imagery Style

Authentic, candid moments of joy and togetherness. Real people in real places — not staged. Always bright, high contrast, and warm. Avoid desaturated or moody photography. The product appears naturally in the scene, never forced.

06 — Core Values

What We Believe

Four principles that guide every decision, from product to pixel.

01

Refresh the World

Every product, experience, and interaction should leave people feeling better than before. Refreshment is both a physical promise and an emotional one.

02

Create Real Magic

Inspire moments of genuine optimism and happiness — not manufactured, not forced, always real. Magic lives in the small moments that make life worth celebrating.

03

Stay Radically Human

Speak plainly, act warmly, and never lose sight of the people behind every sip. We are not a corporation talking at customers — we are people talking to people.

04

Belong Everywhere

Coca-Cola transcends culture, language, and generation. Inclusion is not a stance — it is our nature. No sip has ever asked for credentials.

07 — Brand Introduction

"More than a beverage. A feeling encoded into culture."

Coca-Cola is more than a beverage — it is a feeling encoded into culture. Since 1886, a single red can has carried the weight of celebrations, quiet afternoons, first dates, and stadium roars, becoming the most recognised symbol of shared joy on earth. The brand does not shout its values; it lives them — in the warmth of its red, the familiarity of its script, and the universal truth that happiness is best when shared. Coca-Cola speaks without barriers: to the child buying their first bottle, to the grandparent who has savoured every decade, to everyone in between. It is refreshment, yes — but more profoundly, it is connection. That is the promise Coca-Cola has made for over a century, and the promise it renews with every sip.
